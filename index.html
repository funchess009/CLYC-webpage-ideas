About Us

The Cold Lake Youth Council is a youth-led, volunteer-based organization that acts as peer advocates on civic and social issues. It is composed of youth ages 12-18 who live, work or attend school in Cold Lake. The CLYC represents a community investing in its youth, who are engaged and feel valued in our community. The CLYC was developed in March 2014, and has been running strong since. To join CLYC or attend a meeting as a guest, please contact Cold Lake and District FCSS at 780-594-4495 or email fcss@coldlake.com. For additional details, please refer to the CLYC handbook.

Roles & Responsibilities

Mayor
  • Attending regular and special meetings of the Youth Council
  • Direction and control the operation of the Council
  • Come prepared to meetings with all materials
  • Acting as the spokesperson for the activities of the Youth Council
Deputy Mayor
  • Fulfilling the duties of the Mayor in their absence
Secretary
  • Attendance of all regular or special meetings of any sub-committees of the Youth Council as required
  • Recording and distribution of minutes of such meetings
  • Performing all other duties as may be assigned by the Council from time to time
Treasurer
  • Ensuring that any expenses to the Council budget are recorded and tracked
  • Reports current budget and expenses during regular Council meetings
All Members
  • Attending meetings and being an active member of the Youth Council
  • Helping decide what social activities are needed in the community
  • Acting as a role model to other youth and young members of the community
  • Making a difference for youth in the community
  • Enhancing insight into the needs of youth in the community and helping develop social policy to create a healthy environment for youth
  • Learning leadership skills from the council and how to put these skills into action

Scholarships

Why scholarships are important?

Scholarships are money (Usually around 1-2,000 dollars but sometimes more or less) given to students from organisations to help pay off their tuition fees going into post-secondary schools. Some are through the government, local organisations, sometimes even universities or colleges themselves. To continue, scholarships can also be extremely useful if you're a student who might struggle getting into postsecondary for financial reasons, they provide a great pathway to higher education. To apply for most scholarships you have to either write an essay with who you are, how you're applicable to this scholarship and why you should win. Or in a few cases submit a video with the same information. Overall, scholarships are a small bit of money donated to a student based on academic success or other achievements to help them later in post secondary school.
